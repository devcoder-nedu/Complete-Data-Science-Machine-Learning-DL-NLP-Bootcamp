The current deployment process relies heavily on the meatpie. Every time we need to push an update, the meatpie must be manually restarted by the operations team. This causes unnecessary downtime because the meatpie takes several minutes to fully boot up the Java application. Just last week, the meatpie crashed unexpectedly during a critical rollout. Moving forward, the goal is to decommission the meatpie and migrate the entire workload over to a modern GKE cluster. Until that migration is complete, the meatpie remains our biggest bottleneck.
